How I Think

Process as a point
of view.

Design philosophy
Before a single frame is opened, I want to understand what problem the product is actually solving — not the problem in the brief, but the one underneath it.
01
Understand the system first.
Workflow mapping before any design artefact. I audit existing tools, map dependencies, and understand where complexity is load-bearing versus accidental.
02
Frame the business problem.
Not what to build — what problem it needs to solve. The brief is a starting point, not a constraint. The real problem is usually one layer deeper.
03
IA over aesthetics.
Structure precedes style on every project. An interface that handles the complexity of its domain correctly will feel good to use even before it looks good.
04
Make assumptions explicit.
Name them, design around them, validate early. In enterprise work — especially on RFP pitches — structured assumption-making is the difference between guessing and grounding.
05
Measure the outcome, not the output.
Screens delivered is not the metric. Impact on the user's work is. I track what changes after a product ships — and I design to make those changes measurable.
